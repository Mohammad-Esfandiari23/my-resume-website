Mohammad Esfandiari

Neuroscience Researcher | Data Analysis & Cognitive Science

I am a neuroscience researcher with a strong foundation in cognitive science, psychology, and computational modeling. My research focuses on neural mechanisms underlying perception, decision-making, and time processing. I have experience in designing and conducting behavioral experiments, particularly in the domain of time perception and psychophysics.

My expertise lies in quantitative data analysis and the application of MATLAB and Python for processing behavioral and experimental data. I have worked extensively with PsychToolbox to develop psychophysical tasks and analyze human cognitive responses. My research integrates statistical and computational methods to better understand cognitive and behavioral dynamics.

In the upcoming academic term, I have been offered a Teaching Assistant (TA) position for courses in programming and neuroscience. I will be assisting in instructional material development and guiding students through computational approaches in cognitive science.
